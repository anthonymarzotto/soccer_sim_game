Player Finances — System Specification

This document defines the mathematical equations, multipliers, and logic backing player cash valuations and wage point calculations. Both mechanics are designed to be purely deterministic, side-effect free, and driven entirely by player overalls, ages, and positions. It also documents how those values are used by club budgets, wage caps, transfer listings, and transfer offer resolution.

1. Player Market Value System

Instead of relying on a flat OVR lookup, Market Value uses a Blended Effective OVR to incorporate developmental potential for younger prospects, followed by a piecewise exponential curve gated by age phases and position profiles.

Effective OVR Formula

// 1. Headroom gap realization rate (75%) projectedOvr = currentOvr + (potential - currentOvr) * 0.75;

// 2. Career segment position (0.0 at age 16, 1.0 at peakEndAge) arcPosition = (age - 16) / (peakEndAge - 16); // clamped [0.0, 1.0]

// 3. Blend OVR (under peakEndAge) effectiveOvr = (arcPosition * currentOvr) + ((1.0 - arcPosition) * projectedOvr);

Market Value Base Curve & Floor

exponent = effectiveOvr >= 70 ? 0.2119 : 0.12;
baseValue = 500000 * exp(exponent * (effectiveOvr - 70));

// Final calculation with career arc, position, and floor clamp marketValue = baseValue * careerArcMultiplier * positionMultiplier;
marketValue = Math.max(10000, Math.round(marketValue));

2. Player Wage Cost System

Player wages are represented in points to simplify squad payroll management. The base wage scales exponentially with OVR, modified by discrete career phase and position multipliers, and rounded strictly to the nearest 0.5 points.

Wage Formula

baseWage = 0.005249 * exp(0.0828 * OVR);

rawWage = baseWage * phaseMultiplier * positionMultiplier;

// Round to nearest 0.5 points, clamp to 0.5 minimum wageCost = Math.max(0.5, Math.round(rawWage * 2) / 2);

Wage Points At-A-Glance

• 55 OVR Player (Junior): 0.5 points (League Minimum)

• 70 OVR Player (Peak Midfielder): 1.5 points

• 95 OVR Player (Peak Forward): 15.0 points

3. Career Multipliers & Multiplier Anchors

Career value multipliers scale dynamically as a player progresses through their lifecycle. Market Value uses a continuous Career Arc Multiplier interpolated across five anchor points, while Wages use discrete phase multipliers.

Career Stage Age Range / Points Market Value Multiplier Wage Multiplier
Junior / Start Age 16 to player-specific juniorEndAge Starts at 0.35x, interpolates linearly to 0.82x 0.80x (Flat)
Peak juniorEndAge to peakEndAge Interpolates from 0.82x to 1.00x 1.00x (Flat)
Senior peakEndAge to seniorEndAge Interpolates from 1.00x to 0.68x 0.95x (Flat)
Decline seniorEndAge to Age 42 (Terminal) Interpolates from 0.68x down to 0.20x 0.75x (Flat)

4. Position Multipliers

Position multipliers are applied symmetrically across both Market Value and Wages to model real-world market demands for specific positional roles.

Position Multiplier Value Rationale
Forward (FWD) 1.10x Premium pricing for goalscoring capability.
Midfielder (MID) 1.00x Standard market baseline.
Defender (DEF) 0.90x Defensive roles sit slightly below offensive baseline premium.
Goalkeeper (GK) 0.85x Most specialized position, with historically lower market pricing relative to outfield counterparts.

5. Club Tiers & Financial Constraints

Clubs in the league are sorted descending by starter overall strength and assigned to one of five fixed financial tiers. Tiers enforce strict boundaries on both spending capital (Transfer Budget cash) and ongoing overhead limits (Wage Points caps).

Accessible via the main sidebar under League Finances, this page provides a dynamically sortable, league-wide breakdown of all 20 clubs. Users can contrast and sort clubs on OVR, Tier, Transfer Budget, Squad Value, and Wage Point utilization percentage.

Team Financials Card

Exposed directly in the left-sidebar of any team's details page. It renders the club's active Tier badge, Transfer Budget, dynamically computed total Squad Value (sum of all roster player valuations), and a color-coded wage points utilization meter.

6. Transfer Market & Offer Resolution

The Transfer Market converts player valuation and wage calculations into executable roster transactions. Users can list their own players, bid on listed players from other clubs, and review incoming CPU bids for user-listed players while a transfer window is active.

User Bid Flow & Asking Price

Transfer offers are blocked outside active transfer windows and cannot target players already on the user's team. The bid modal defaults the offer amount to 115% of Market Value, displays the user's Transfer Budget, current Wage Headroom, and the target player's Wage Point Cost. Submissions are disabled if the bid exceeds available cash or the player's wage exceeds remaining cap space. The seller's asking price depends on listed status: listed players require a flat 1.15x premium. Unlisted players command a premium multiplier based on OVR relative to positional peers on their roster: Starters (1.40x to 1.60x), Bench (1.25x to 1.35x), and Reserves (1.15x).

Incoming CPU Offers

CPU clubs evaluate user-listed players during active windows and may create pending offers if they can afford the bid, have enough wage headroom, and the player improves their positional depth or youth value. Incoming offers appear above the listed-player table with Accept and Decline actions. CPU teams bid up to 1.15x of the player's market value.

CPU-to-CPU Transfers

During active transfer windows, CPU clubs dynamically trade with each other weekly. A CPU club has a 40% weekly chance to search the listings. It targets its weakest positions based on roster depth and overall quality, purchasing the best-matching listed player if they satisfy budget constraints, wage cap limits, and improve the squad. To prevent instant re-sale chains, CPU teams cannot list or auto-list players acquired in the current season.

Offer Status Lifecycle

Status Meaning Primary Trigger
pending Offer is awaiting a user decision. CPU bid generated for a user-listed player.
accepted Offer caused a completed player transfer. User accepts an incoming bid, or a user bid clears all validation.
rejected Offer was refused and remains only as history. User declines a CPU bid, or a user bid is below the seller's asking price / roster-depth floor. Rejection reasons return vague flavor text (e.g. "demands a substantial premium") instead of the exact figure.
expired Offer is no longer actionable. Transfer window closes, the target player transfers elsewhere, or buyer budget/wage headroom becomes invalid.

Transfer Execution Rules

// Buyer affordability checks buyer.transferBudget >= offer.fee;
buyer.wagePointsCap - buyer.wagePointsUsed >= playerWageCost;

// Seller roster safety floor before selling GK >= 1, DEF >= 3, MID >= 3, FWD >= 2;

// Accepted transfer mutations (Buyer pays full fee; Seller receives 90% after 10% agent commission) buyer.transferBudget -= fee;
seller.transferBudget += Math.round(fee * 0.9);
buyer.wagePointsUsed = recalculateSquadWages(buyer.players);
seller.wagePointsUsed = recalculateSquadWages(seller.players);

Persisted Transfer Metadata

League metadata stores transferOffers and evaluatedCpuOfferPlayerIds alongside transferListings. Player records can store transferHistory entries containing seller team, buyer team, fee, season year, and week.

Schema Impact

The persisted transfer fields are part of data schema version N. Existing persisted leagues from older schema versions should be treated as incompatible until regenerated or migrated through the app's schema version safeguards.

7. Season Rollover & Economic Stabilizers

To prevent hyper-inflation and maintain league financial equilibrium over multiple seasons, two stabilizers are applied at the end of each season: Transfer Budget Decay and Prize Money Allocation.

Transfer Budget Decay

Prior to awarding prize money at the end of a season, all clubs decay their unspent transfer budgets by 20% (meaning they retain 80% of their existing budget). This decay establishes a progressive upper limit on club wealth accumulation, preventing top-tier clubs from hoarding unlimited cash.

Prize Money Formula

Season-end prize money is awarded to all teams based on their final league standings rank:

BASE_PRIZE = $100,000;
RANK_BONUS = $50,000;

prizeMoney = BASE_PRIZE + (totalTeams - rank) * RANK_BONUS;

// Final rollover budget calculation nextSeasonBudget = (currentBudget * 0.8) + prizeMoney;

Economic Equilibrium

Under this system, the maximum theoretical budget for a team is limited to 5x their annual prize money. For example, the league champion (1st place) can reach a maximum long-term transfer budget of $5,250,000 (assuming a 20-team league), while the 20th place team is capped at $500,000. This naturally keeps budgets aligned with player valuations.

Transaction Tax

A 10% agent commission fee is deducted from the seller's proceeds on every transfer. While the buying club pays 100% of the negotiated fee, the selling club only receives 90%. This acts as an additional deflationary sink, removing currency from the game's economy on every transaction.
