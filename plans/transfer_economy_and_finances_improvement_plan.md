# Transfer Economy, Wage Caps & Positional Valuation Improvement Plan

## Executive Summary

Based on a **20-Season Simulation Audit** (over 7,600 simulated match weeks and seasonal transitions), this document outlines empirical findings regarding the game's transfer market, financial mechanics, wage ratios, roster dynamics, and goalkeeper valuations, followed by a consolidated 7-point improvement plan.

---

## 20-Season Simulation Findings

### 1. Wage Cap Bottleneck & Luxury Tax Debt Trap
- **Day 1 Wage Cap Excess**: In Season 1, 11 of 20 teams (55%) are maxed out or over their wage cap immediately upon league generation.
- **Structural Debt Spiral**: By Seasons 5–10, average wage utilization reaches **104%–106%**, with up to 11 teams operating over their wage cap.
- **Luxury Tax Impact**: Weekly luxury tax penalties (`Math.round(excess * 10000)`) combined with 20% annual budget decay reduce average lower-tier transfer budgets from **+$0.87M in Season 1** down to **-$1.19M in Season 20**.
- **Asymmetric Wealth Concentration**: Top-tier teams (Tier 1) remain under their wage cap, collecting ~$1.05M in annual prize money, accumulating $10M–$20M in cash. Lower-tier teams fall into deep negative debt (-$2M to -$5M), creating market polarization.

### 2. Roster Composition & Hoarding
- **Squad Size Divergence**: Squad sizes diverge from a uniform 25 players in Season 1 to **18–40 players** by Seasons 10–15. Top teams hoard up to 40 players while cash-strapped teams drop to minimum depth floors.
- **Positional Imbalance**: CPU auto-listing sell floors hold goalkeepers at ~2 per team while defenders (~7–8), midfielders (~6–7), and forwards (~8–9) fluctuate based on wage efficiency listings.

### 3. Goalkeeper Rating & Value Distortion
- **OVR Bias**: Average Goalkeeper OVR is **71–72**, compared to **63–66 for Forwards**, **59–64 for Midfielders**, and **57–59 for Defenders**.
- **Root Cause**: `POSITION_OVR_CONFIG` in `src/app/models/player-progression.ts` defines only **2 core attributes** (`handling`, `reflexes`) for GKs. High baseline values in these 2 stats result in artificially inflated 70+ OVRs, whereas outfield positions blend 5 core stats plus 12+ tertiary stats.
- **Economic Impact**:
  - GK Market Values average **$4.4M–$6.1M** (3x to 8x higher than defenders at $0.52M–$1.38M).
  - GK Wages average **2.02–2.13 pts** (compared to defenders at 0.86–1.04 pts).
  - GK Wage/OVR ratio is **0.030**—nearly double the outfield defender ratio (0.018).

## Consolidated 7-Point Improvement Plan

### 1. Initial Tier Wage Cap Calibration
- **Location**: `src/app/services/generator.service.ts` (`tierConfig`)
- **Change**: Re-align initial tier wage caps to account for the actual wages generated by 25-man rosters:
  - Tier 5: Increase `cap` from `17` to `22`
  - Tier 4: Increase `cap` from `21` to `25`
  - Tier 3: Increase `cap` from `29` to `33`
- **Goal**: Ensure newly generated leagues start with ~10–15% wage headroom rather than immediate Day 1 luxury tax debt.

### 2. Luxury Tax Clamping & Debt Protection
- **Location**: `src/app/services/game.service.ts` (`advanceWeek`)
- **Change**: Clamp weekly luxury tax penalties:
  - Implement a maximum weekly tax cap (e.g., max 2.0 wage points excess penalty or max $20,000/week).
  - Pause or scale down luxury tax deductions when a team's transfer budget reaches a floor threshold (e.g., -$500,000).
- **Goal**: Prevent lower-tier CPU teams from falling into an irreversible multi-million dollar negative budget spiral.

### 3. Automated CPU Emergency Player Release
- **Location**: `src/app/services/game.service.ts` (`advanceWeek` / `runCpuAutoListingForLeague`)
- **Change**: Add an emergency player release check during season transition or weekly processing:
  - If a CPU team is over its wage cap and has negative transfer budget, automatically release 3rd-string reserve players (above positional sell floors) to free agent pool.
- **Goal**: Allow bankrupt teams to restore financial solvency rather than holding 30–40 players while accumulating luxury tax penalties.

### 4. Hard Squad Size Limits
- **Location**: `src/app/services/game.service.ts` & `src/app/services/generator.service.ts`
- **Change**: Enforce strict squad size bounds:
  - Minimum Roster: 18 players
  - Maximum Roster: 30 players
- **Goal**: Prevent wealthy top-tier teams from hoarding 40+ players and keep all team rosters balanced.

### 5. Goalkeeper OVR Formula & Valuation Recalibration
- **Location**: `src/app/models/player-progression.ts` (`POSITION_OVR_CONFIG` & `calculateMarketValue` / `calculatePlayerMarketWageCost`)
- **Change**:
  - Expand GK core config in `POSITION_OVR_CONFIG` to include `commandOfArea` and `shortPassing` (or adjust core weights).
  - Apply a GK positional multiplier/scaling factor to align baseline GK OVR distribution (~60–65) with outfield positions.
- **Goal**: Normalize goalkeeper market values ($1.5M–$2.5M) and wage costs (~1.0–1.3 pts) to prevent GKs from consuming disproportionate squad payrolls.

### 6. Dynamic Wage Cap Indexing
- **Location**: `src/app/services/game.service.ts` (`startNewSeason`)
- **Change**: Index team `wagePointsCap` over long-term seasons:
  - Allow team wage caps to adjust dynamically based on tier placement, annual league revenue, or long-term league growth (+1–2% per season).
- **Goal**: Prevent fixed wage caps from causing league-wide wage stagnation after 10+ seasons of player progression.

### 7. CPU Transfer Buyer Financial Balancing
- **Location**: `src/app/services/game.service.ts` (`runCpuToCpuTransferPass`)
- **Change**: Refine CPU buyer evaluation logic:
  - Prevent top-tier teams from buying non-essential high-wage backup players simply because they have excess budget.
  - Require CPU buying decisions to strictly weigh wage cap headroom vs player squad role (STARTER vs BENCH).
- **Goal**: Reduce price inflation on auto-listed players and encourage more balanced transfer activity across all tiers.
